Crime and Punishment

Crime and Punishment

Story: Crime and Punishment is a psychological drama that follows Rodion Raskolnikov, a poor ex-student in Saint Petersburg who formulates a plan to kill an unscrupulous pawnbroker for her money. The novel explores themes of morality, redemption, and the psychological effects of guilt.

Main Characters: Rodion Raskolnikov, Sonia Marmeladova, Arkady Svidrigailov

Main Themes: Moral dilemmas, redemption, nihilism

The Brothers Karamazov

The Brothers Karamazov

Story: The Brothers Karamazov is a philosophical novel set in 19th-century Russia, exploring the themes of faith, doubt, and reason through the lives of the Karamazov brothers: Dmitri, Ivan, and Alexei, and their father Fyodor Pavlovich.

Main Characters: Dmitri Karamazov, Ivan Karamazov, Alexei Karamazov, Fyodor Pavlovich Karamazov

Main Themes: Faith vs. doubt, father-son relationships, free will vs. determinism

Demons

Demons

Story: Demons (also known as The Possessed or The Devils) is a novel that examines political extremism and nihilism in 19th-century Russia. It follows the descent of a provincial town into chaos after the arrival of a revolutionary agitator, Pyotr Verkhovensky.

Main Characters: Pyotr Stepanovich Verkhovensky, Nikolai Stavrogin, Stepan Trofimovich Verkhovensky

Main Themes: Political radicalism, nihilism, moral decay
